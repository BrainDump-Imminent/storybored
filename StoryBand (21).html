Read this aloud
Read clearly and naturally — the way you'd tell a bedtime story. We need at least 60 seconds.
Once upon a time, in a land just beyond the evening star, there lived a child made entirely of curiosity and warmth. Each night they would wander through a forest of gentle sounds — leaves rustling, owls whispering, water moving over smooth stones. They believed that every story was true, and every true thing was also a little bit magical. The moon agreed with them completely. Stars do too, when nobody is watching closely enough.

So they would lie down in the soft dark and let the stories find them — stories about brave little creatures who learned surprising things, about friends who helped each other across rivers that had lost their bridges, about the particular happiness of being read to by someone who loves you very much. And every night, without fail, the stories were there.
